Arkisto is a scaleable, standards based platform for sustainable data. Built on top of Research Object Crate (RO-Crate) and the Oxford Common Filesystem Layout (OCFL) Arkisto enables research services to support diverse research domains ranging linguistics to bioinformatics.

The basis of Arkisto is that the long-term preservability of well-described data is *always* the first consideration - that data on an Arkisto deployment is alway available on disc (or object storage) with a complete description _independently_ of any services, such as websites or APIs. This is an example of a data commons, and tests this notion by incorporating a range of datatypes from diverse disciplinary communities.

Once the data is safe and well described, Arkisto has a flexible model for how data can be accessed using a variety of services:

  • Human and machine oriented access-controlled data portals that make data available by searching and browsing and by API. There are currently two established portal projects and a number of active developments.
  • Direct access by trusted services which can read and write data from the storage layer.
  • Validation and preservation services at the data storage layer. There are currently very few of these.
